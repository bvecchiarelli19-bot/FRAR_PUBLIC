UNDER THE HOOD

Transparent methodology.
No black boxes.

For investors who want to understand exactly what drives every alert. SteadyGuard is fully transparent. Here's how it works at the technical level.

Metric 1

Volatility Percentile

Where is current volatility relative to its own history? Higher percentile = more risk. Measures the clustering of large moves.

Metric 2

Tail Risk (Hill Estimator)

How likely is an extreme move? The Hill estimator measures the "fatness" of distribution tails. Fatter tails = higher crash probability.

Metric 3

Trend Strength (Hurst Exponent)

Is the market trending or choppy? A Hurst value near 0.5 means random. Below 0.5 means mean-reverting. Above means trending, which can amplify drawdowns.

Metric 4

Trend Filter (10-Month MA)

Is the market above or below its long-term trend? When prices drop below the 10-month moving average, the system increases defensive positioning automatically.

These four metrics are combined into a single composite risk score, ranked against its own history, and mapped to the four allocation levels above. The entire process is rules-based with no human discretion. Full methodology documentation available to subscribers.

FAQ

Common questions

Is this market timing?

No. Market timing tries to predict where prices will go. SteadyGuard measures current market conditions: how volatile things are, how likely a big move is, and whether prices are trending. It's like checking the weather forecast before you leave the house. We're not predicting a storm. We're telling you it's already getting cloudy.

What do I actually do with the alert?

Once a month, you check the alert. If it says 80%, you move 80% of your investment portfolio into stocks (or a stock index fund) and put the rest in bonds or cash. That's it. One adjustment, once a month. Most months, nothing changes.

What if SteadyGuard is wrong?

It will be sometimes. SteadyGuard can underperform during calm markets that keep rising, and it can't react to sudden shocks that happen between monthly alerts. It's designed to reduce average drawdowns across full market cycles, not to be perfect every month. We publish all known limitations for full transparency.

How much does it cost?

The monthly alert (Calm / Cautious / Risky / Crisis) will always be free. That's the core insight. Premium features like detailed analysis, email alerts, the portfolio backtest tool, and historical data access will be available through a subscription. Pricing will be announced when we launch.
